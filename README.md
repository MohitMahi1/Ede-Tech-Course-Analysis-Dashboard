## 1. Project Title & Headline
**Online Course Analysis Dashboard**  
*Category-wise insights to drive strategic growth in recorded lecture offerings*

---

## 2. Description & Purpose
This project analyses online course data collected from multiple EdTech platforms to help an EdTech startup expand and optimise its **recorded lecture offerings**. Using **Power BI**, the dataset was cleaned, modelled, and visualised to extract actionable insights related to learner engagement, content strategy, language preferences, instructor performance, and skill demand.

The dashboard strongly emphasises **category-wise analysis**, enabling business stakeholders to take informed, data-driven decisions.

## 6. Business Problem
The EdTech startup wants to scale its recorded lecture offerings but lacks clarity on:
- Which **categories and sub-categories** drive the highest engagement  
- What **skills** are currently in demand  
- Preferred **languages** for content consumption  
- The influence of **course duration** and **subtitles** on viewership  
- Which **instructors** consistently deliver high-quality content  

---

## 7. Goal of Making This Dashboard
- Support strategic planning for recorded lecture growth  
- Identify high-performing categories and content formats  
- Improve learner engagement through accessibility insights  
- Align skill offerings with job-market trends  
- Highlight top instructors for potential collaboration

## 8. Key Visuals Used (Actual Dashboard)

1. **Category Navigation Buttons (Slicers)**  
   Used for switching between major categories such as Business, Data Science, Health, IT, Language Learning, etc., enabling focused category-wise analysis.

2. **Horizontal Bar Chart ‚Äì Course Type Popularity**  
   Displays the count of courses by course type (Course, Specialization, Professional Certificate).

3. **Clustered Column Chart ‚Äì Average Number of Viewers by Sub-Category**  
   Visualises average views across sub-categories to measure learner engagement.

4. **Word Cloud ‚Äì Demanding Skills**  
   Highlights the most commonly taught and in-demand skills such as Python, Data Analysis, and Machine Learning.

5. **Table ‚Äì Category-wise Average Views**  
   Used to rank and compare categories based on viewer engagement.

6. **Pie Chart ‚Äì Most Prominent Language**  
   Shows language distribution of courses, highlighting dominant content languages.

7. **Line Chart ‚Äì Number of Viewers per Subtitle Count**  
   Examines the relationship between subtitle availability and number of views.

8. **Sub-Category Slicer**  
   Enables filtering insights for specific sub-categories like Algorithms, Biology, Cloud Computing, etc.

9. **Bar Chart ‚Äì Instructor Rating by Instructor (Static Visual)**  
   Identifies top-rated instructors delivering consistently high-quality content.

10. **Line Chart ‚Äì Viewership Based on Lecture Duration**  
    Analyses how course duration (in hours) affects learner engagement.

11. **Table ‚Äì Category-wise Skill Variety & Course Duration**  
    Displays average skill count and average course duration to study their impact on viewership.

---

## 9. Business Impact & Insights
- High-demand categories and sub-categories are clearly identified  
- English dominates as the primary content language  
- Courses with subtitles show higher viewer engagement  
- Optimal course duration ranges improve retention  
- Skill diversity positively influences viewership  
- Top-performing instructors can be targeted for collaboration
